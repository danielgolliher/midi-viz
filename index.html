MIDI Visualizer

Abstract mode: every note blooms. Triads spawn geometric bursts; arpeggios leave flowing trails. Color follows the circle of fifths.

Painting mode: hold a triad and a matching public-domain painting (Met Open Access) fades in — chosen to match the character musicians hear in each triad:

  • Major — bright, resolved: Hudson River luminism (Church, Bierstadt, Kensett, Lane).
  • Minor — pensive, melancholy: Tonalism (Inness, Whistler, Tryon, Twachtman).
  • Diminished — tense, unresolved: storms and shipwrecks (Homer, Ryder, Allston).
  • Augmented — dreamlike, suspended: symbolists and allegorists (Cole, Vedder, Blakelock, La Farge).

Controls: click the toggle to switch modes. Press Space to toggle, ? for this help, Esc to close.

No MIDI piano handy? Use your QWERTY keyboard. White keys: A S D F G H J K L ;. Black keys: W E   T Y U   O P. Z/X shift octave.

Paintings load once from the Met Museum Open Access API and are cached in your browser. First switch into Painting mode may take a few seconds.

On iPad: Safari 18 or newer has Web MIDI. If the indicator stays red/amber after plugging in, tap Connect MIDI to grant permission (some browsers require a user gesture).
